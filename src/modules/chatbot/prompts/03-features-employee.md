## Features Available to You

- **Home**: View today's attendance status, clock-in/clock-out status, and WFH flag. This is also where you submit new requests via Quick Action cards. If you have not yet registered your face (or your KYC was rejected), a **notification banner** will appear at the top of the Home page with a link to your Profile to complete registration. The Home page also contains a **team calendar** that shows approved and pending WFH, Leave, Overtime, and Business Trip requests from all colleagues. You can toggle between **month view** and **week view**. Pending requests appear in italic (with a dashed indicator) so you can distinguish them from approved ones. Click any date on the calendar to open a detail panel showing who is WFH, on leave, working overtime, or on a business trip that day (business trip entries also show the destination). The Home page also shows a **Birthday Highlight banner** when any colleague has a birthday this month — the banner lists upcoming birthdays in the month and spotlights anyone whose birthday is today. When a colleague's birthday falls on the current day, a **birthday notification popup** automatically appears with their name and a 🎊 celebration; the popup is dismissed for the rest of the day once you close it.
- **My Requests**: View all your submitted requests. You can **Edit** pending requests or **Reuse** a past request to submit again (reuse pre-fills the form with the previous values). There is no create button here — to create a request go to the Home page. Use the **month/year selectors** at the top to browse different periods, the **status filter** (All / Pending / Approved / Rejected), and the **type tabs** (All / WFH / Leave / Overtime / Equipment / Clock Forget) to narrow down your list. Click **Export Excel** to download your filtered request list as a spreadsheet. The list updates in real-time when a request is approved or rejected by an admin.
- **Profile**: View your personal information (name, position, email, username, roles, phone, address, date of birth, employment details) and update it via the Edit page. You can also change your password from the Security tab on the Edit page. The employment info section also shows your **annual leave hours** and **remaining leave hours**. The profile shows your **contract info** — contract type (Probation / Fixed-term / Indefinite), contract number, signed date, and expiry date (if set). The Profile page also contains a **Face KYC** panel where you can register your face for face-based attendance check-in. After submitting, an admin must approve your KYC before face check-in is enabled. If your KYC is rejected, you will see the rejection reason and can re-submit. Once approved, a **"Face Verified"** badge appears on your profile. The Profile page also shows your **Attendance History** — a monthly table with each day's scheduled shift hours (start–end), clock-in time, clock-out time, and Present/Absent status. Use the month and year selectors at the top of the table to browse different months.
- **Chat**: A real-time messaging feature accessible from the sidebar. The room list has two tabs: **"Của tôi" (My Rooms)** — shows rooms you have already joined, split into **Channels** (group rooms) and **Direct Messages** (1-on-1 conversations); unread rooms appear first. **"Khám phá" (Discover)** shows public channels you can browse and join. You can also **create a new room** — choose type **Channel** (group, with name, description, and optional private/public visibility) or **Direct** (1-on-1 with a specific person). When creating a channel you can invite members and entire teams at creation time. **Sections**: Organize your room list into named sections using the **"Manage Sections"** button at the top of the room list. Once sections exist, move any room into a section via the ⋮ menu → **"Move to section"**. Each section header can be collapsed or expanded by clicking it. Inside a room you can send rich text messages using the **formatting toolbar** (toggle it with the toolbar button): **Bold** (Ctrl+B), **Italic** (Ctrl+I), **Underline**, **Strikethrough**, **Inline Code**, **Code Block**, **Blockquote**, **Bullet List**, and **Ordered List**. Type **@name** to mention a member — an autocomplete dropdown appears; navigate with arrow keys or click to insert. Mentioned users receive a highlighted notification. A **typing indicator** appears below the message list when other room members are actively typing. Reply in threads, react to messages with emoji (including animated custom emoji packs like blob, meow, froge, parrots, and more). The emoji picker remembers your recently used emoji in a dedicated **Recent** tab for quick access. Only one reaction per message per user is allowed — clicking a different emoji replaces your current reaction; clicking the same emoji removes it. You can also **pin a message** — any room member can pin, but only room admins can unpin. When messages are pinned, a **pinned messages bar** appears at the top of the room; click it to view the full list of pinned messages. Click the **members icon** (top-right of the room) to open the **member sidebar** showing online and offline members with their status. Room admins can **kick** a member directly from this sidebar. When someone shares a photo or an entire album from Memories, the message appears as an interactive **card** — a photo card shows the image thumbnail and can be clicked to open the photo in the Memories viewer; an **album card** shows the cover photo, album title, photo count, and event type, and clicking it navigates to the full album page.
- **Meetings**: A real-time internal video meeting feature accessible from the sidebar. Meeting rooms are **reusable** — they cycle between Scheduled (idle) and Active (in use) status; there is no Ended status. The meeting list shows rooms with their status, schedule type, and **live participant avatars** for active rooms. Use the **status filter chips** (All / Scheduled / Active) at the top to narrow the list. Click the **pin icon** on any meeting card to pin it to the top of your list; click again to unpin. A **Host** chip appears on cards for meetings you created. You can **create a new meeting** — choose a schedule type (**One-time**, **Daily**, or **Weekly**), select which **companies** can access it, and set visibility to **public** or **private** (private rooms auto-generate a password). As the host, you can **edit** your meeting (title, description, schedule, privacy, companies), **regenerate the password** for private rooms (with a confirmation step), and **delete** the meeting (only when it is not active). Hosts can also manage **host schedules** — configure a rotation so different people host on different dates (schedule types: one-time, date list, date range, or recurring weekly). **Sections**: Organize your meeting list into named sections using the **"Manage Sections"** button at the top of the list. Once sections exist, you can move any meeting card into a section via the ⋮ menu → **"Move to section"**. Each section header can be **collapsed or expanded** by clicking it. Meetings not assigned to any section appear below the named sections grouped by Private / Public. **Scheduled Participants**: From the meeting card ⋮ menu → **"Manage Scheduled Participants"**, the host can pre-invite specific users who should attend. Each invitee receives a notification and must **Accept or Decline** (RSVP). The dialog shows each participant's current RSVP status (Accepted / Declined / Pending). When you have pending RSVP invitations, a **banner** appears at the top of the meeting list — click Accept or Decline directly on the banner. The host can also configure **Auto-call** in this dialog: enable it to have the system automatically call scheduled participants before the meeting starts — configure **minutes before**, **retry count**, **retry interval** (minutes between retries), and whether to **skip weekends**. To join, click **Join** — a pre-join screen lets you preview camera/mic, select devices, and toggle mic, camera, and speaker. Inside the room: **video grid** of all participants, **subtitle panel** with real-time trilingual transcription (original + Vietnamese, English, Japanese translations), and a **control bar** with: mic, camera, screen share (quality options: Video / Balanced / Document), **TTS toggle** (turn text-to-speech read-aloud on/off), **Chat** button (opens an in-room chat panel with thread replies, pinned messages, and auto-translate), vote panel toggle, fullscreen, **Settings** (change camera/mic/speaker device, test mic level with a visual bar), and leave. The **host** sees additional controls: **transfer host** to another participant, **manage co-hosts** (promote participants to co-host or demote them back), and **end meeting** for everyone. The host can promote other participants to **co-host** — co-hosts can **invite people**, **edit the meeting**, **manage host schedules**, and **end the meeting**, but cannot transfer host or manage other co-hosts. The host can also **pre-assign co-hosts from the meeting list** (without joining the room) by clicking the ⋮ menu on a meeting card → **"Manage Co-Hosts"** — this opens a dialog to search users and add/remove co-hosts. **Voting**: any participant can create a vote from the vote panel — choose **Single Choice** (radio), **Multiple Choice** (checkbox), or **Story Points** (Fibonacci cards: 0, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, ?, coffee). Select which participants can vote (or all). The creator can close the vote early; results show counts and percentages (average for story points). **Annotations**: during screen sharing, all participants see each other's **cursors** (color-coded with names) and can **click to place markers** (pulsing circles) on the shared screen. When speaking, your voice is automatically transcribed and translated — the translated text is also read aloud via TTS. In the subtitle panel, use the **speaking language selector** (VI / JA dropdown) to tell the system which language you are speaking; this helps Whisper transcribe more accurately. The selected language is saved in your browser and persists across sessions.
- **Memories**: A shared photo album feature accessible from the sidebar. Browse albums created by you or shared with you. Each album has a **title**, **event type** (Team Building, Birthday, Trip, Award, Launch, or Other), **date**, and **privacy** setting (public — visible to all company members; private — visible only to invited members). You can **create a new album** by providing a title, event type, date, description, and privacy. Inside an album you can **upload photos** (up to 20 at a time), browse the photo grid, and click any photo to open the **photo viewer**. The viewer shows the full-size image with navigation arrows (or swipe left/right on mobile) to move between photos. Inside the viewer: **react** to a photo with emoji (❤️ 🥰 😄 😲 😡 😢) — only one reaction per photo per user; **comment** on the photo, edit or delete your own comments, react to comments with emoji, and **translate** comments to your preferred language. Click **⋮** to share the photo to a chat room, copy a direct link, or delete the photo (album creator or uploader only). On desktop, **double-click** the image to zoom in at that point; double-click again to zoom out. On mobile, **double-click** triggers a ❤️ heart animation. Pinch to zoom is also supported on mobile. Press **Fullscreen** (desktop only) to view the image full-screen. From the album page, click **Share album** to send the entire album to any chat room — members who receive it see an **album card** in chat (cover photo, title, photo count, event type) and can click the card to open the album. Album creators can also **edit album info** (title, event type, date, description, privacy), **manage members** (invite colleagues to a private album), and **download the entire album** as a ZIP file (album creator and members only).
- **Settings** (sidebar): Accessible from the sidebar. Currently contains one section — **Meeting Notifications** — where you can choose your preferred **ringtone** for incoming meeting invites. There are 4 ringtone options, each with a **Preview** button so you can hear the sound before selecting. Your choice is saved automatically and persists across sessions.
- **Report Bug** (sidebar): Click the bug icon in the bottom of the sidebar to report a bug. The system will automatically capture a screenshot of your current screen and attach it. Add a title and optional description, then submit.
