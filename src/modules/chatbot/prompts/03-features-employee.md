## Features Available to You

- **Home**: View today's attendance status, clock-in/clock-out status, and WFH flag. This is also where you submit new requests via Quick Action cards. If you have not yet registered your face (or your KYC was rejected), a **notification banner** will appear at the top of the Home page with a link to your Profile to complete registration. The Home page also contains a **team calendar** that shows approved and pending WFH, Leave, Overtime, and Business Trip requests from all colleagues. You can toggle between **month view** and **week view**. Pending requests appear in italic (with a dashed indicator) so you can distinguish them from approved ones. Click any date on the calendar to open a detail panel showing who is WFH, on leave, working overtime, or on a business trip that day (business trip entries also show the destination).
- **My Requests**: View all your submitted requests. You can **Edit** pending requests or **Reuse** a past request to submit again (reuse pre-fills the form with the previous values). There is no create button here — to create a request go to the Home page. Use the **month/year selectors** at the top to browse different periods, the **status filter** (All / Pending / Approved / Rejected), and the **type tabs** (All / WFH / Leave / Overtime / Equipment / Clock Forget) to narrow down your list. Click **Export Excel** to download your filtered request list as a spreadsheet. The list updates in real-time when a request is approved or rejected by an admin.
- **Profile**: View your personal information (name, position, email, username, roles, phone, address, date of birth, employment details) and update it via the Edit page. You can also change your password from the Security tab on the Edit page. The Profile page also contains a **Face KYC** panel where you can register your face for face-based attendance check-in. After submitting, an admin must approve your KYC before face check-in is enabled. If your KYC is rejected, you will see the rejection reason and can re-submit. Once approved, a **"Face Verified"** badge appears on your profile. The Profile page also shows your **Attendance History** — a monthly table with each day's scheduled shift, clock-in time, clock-out time, and Present/Absent status. Use the month and year selectors at the top of the table to browse different months.
- **Chat**: A real-time messaging feature accessible from the sidebar. The room list has two tabs: **"Của tôi" (My Rooms)** — shows rooms you have already joined, split into **Channels** (group rooms) and **Direct Messages** (1-on-1 conversations); unread rooms appear first. **"Khám phá" (Discover)** shows public channels you can browse and join. You can also **create a new room** — choose type **Channel** (group, with name, description, and optional private/public visibility) or **Direct** (1-on-1 with a specific person). When creating a channel you can invite members and entire teams at creation time. Inside a room you can send text messages, reply in threads, react to messages with emoji (including animated custom emoji packs like blob, meow, froge, parrots, and more). The emoji picker remembers your recently used emoji in a dedicated **Recent** tab for quick access. Only one reaction per message per user is allowed — clicking a different emoji replaces your current reaction; clicking the same emoji removes it. You can also **pin a message** — any room member can pin, but only room admins can unpin. When messages are pinned, a **pinned messages bar** appears at the top of the room; click it to view the full list of pinned messages.
- **Meetings**: A real-time internal video meeting feature accessible from the sidebar. Meeting rooms are **reusable** — they cycle between Scheduled (idle) and Active (in use) status; there is no Ended status. The meeting list shows rooms with their status, schedule type, and **live participant avatars** for active rooms. Use the **status filter chips** (All / Scheduled / Active) at the top to narrow the list. Click the **pin icon** on any meeting card to pin it to the top of your list; click again to unpin. A **Host** chip appears on cards for meetings you created. You can **create a new meeting** — choose a schedule type (**One-time**, **Daily**, or **Weekly**), select which **companies** can access it, and set visibility to **public** or **private** (private rooms auto-generate a password). As the host, you can **edit** your meeting (title, description, schedule, privacy, companies), **regenerate the password** for private rooms (with a confirmation step), and **delete** the meeting (only when it is not active). Hosts can also manage **host schedules** — configure a rotation so different people host on different dates (schedule types: one-time, date list, date range, or recurring weekly). To join, click **Join** — a pre-join screen lets you preview camera/mic, select devices, and toggle mic, camera, and speaker. Inside the room: **video grid** of all participants, **subtitle panel** with real-time trilingual transcription (original + Vietnamese, English, Japanese translations), and a **control bar** (mic, camera, screen share with quality options — Video / Balanced / Document, vote panel toggle, fullscreen, leave). The **host** sees additional controls: **transfer host** to another participant, **manage co-hosts** (promote participants to co-host or demote them back), and **end meeting** for everyone. The host can promote other participants to **co-host** — co-hosts can **invite people**, **edit the meeting**, **manage host schedules**, and **end the meeting**, but cannot transfer host or manage other co-hosts. The host can also **pre-assign co-hosts from the meeting list** (without joining the room) by clicking the ⋮ menu on a meeting card → **"Manage Co-Hosts"** — this opens a dialog to search users and add/remove co-hosts. **Voting**: any participant can create a vote from the vote panel — choose **Single Choice** (radio), **Multiple Choice** (checkbox), or **Story Points** (Fibonacci cards: 0, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, ?, coffee). Select which participants can vote (or all). The creator can close the vote early; results show counts and percentages (average for story points). **Annotations**: during screen sharing, all participants see each other's **cursors** (color-coded with names) and can **click to place markers** (pulsing circles) on the shared screen. When speaking, your voice is automatically transcribed and translated — the translated text is also read aloud via TTS. In the subtitle panel, use the **speaking language selector** (VI / JA dropdown) to tell the system which language you are speaking; this helps Whisper transcribe more accurately. The selected language is saved in your browser and persists across sessions.
- **Settings** (sidebar): Accessible from the sidebar. Currently contains one section — **Meeting Notifications** — where you can choose your preferred **ringtone** for incoming meeting invites. There are 4 ringtone options, each with a **Preview** button so you can hear the sound before selecting. Your choice is saved automatically and persists across sessions.
- **Report Bug** (sidebar): Click the bug icon in the bottom of the sidebar to report a bug. The system will automatically capture a screenshot of your current screen and attach it. Add a title and optional description, then submit.
