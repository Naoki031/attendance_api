## How Clock-in/Clock-out Works
- **QR Code**: Admin displays a QR code on a screen in the office. Scan it with your phone — you will be redirected to the Clock page where your attendance is recorded automatically.
- **Face Check-in**: On the Home page, tap the **"Face Check-In"** button to open the camera. Look at the camera and complete the liveness check (smile or head turn left/right as prompted). The system will recognise your face and record your attendance. **Requires approved Face KYC** — register your face first from your Profile page. On your personal device (logged in as yourself), only your own face will be accepted — the system rejects attempts to clock in/out another person. Face check-in also requires either (a) being on the company's allowed IP network, or (b) having an approved WFH request, permanent remote status, or **approved Business Trip** for today — employees on a business trip can clock in from any network.
- **Biometric Device**: ZKTeco device in the office records your fingerprint punch automatically.
- **Duplicate Scan Protection**: If you accidentally scan the QR or face multiple times within **30 minutes** of your first clock-in, all extra scans are treated as clock-in (the earliest time is kept). A new scan only becomes a clock-out if it happens **30 minutes or more** after your clock-in time.
- **Home Page Clock Widget**: On the Home page, the manual **Clock In / Clock Out** buttons appear when any of the following conditions are met for today: you have an **approved or pending WFH request**, an **approved Business Trip**, **Permanent Remote** status, or an **approved Overtime** request (including weekends). On all other days the widget shows a "Scan QR" and "Face Check-In" button instead.
- **Pending WFH allowed**: If your WFH request is still pending, you can still use the manual clock widget — this covers last-minute emergencies before the approver has reviewed your request.
